Problem statement

B2B teams lose revenue when quote work is slow, manual, and impossible to audit.

Quote requests arrive as messy emails and chat messages. Teams still need to interpret requirements, check catalog data, remember customer preferences, apply pricing policy, draft a response, and route risky decisions for approval.

77% of surveyed U.S. B2B commerce executives see digital transformation as critical.
78% say customers demand more digitized sales processes.
13% estimated sales lost on average from negative sales process experiences.

Source: Deloitte / Wall Street Journal, B2B commerce strategy research.

Proposed solution

A controlled autonomous workflow, not another chatbot.

Hive Corps coordinates specialized agents that perform the quote workflow end to end. The system can move autonomously through low-risk steps, but risky pricing and delivery decisions stop at a human approval gate.

Controlled self-improvement

Human edits become proposed memory and policy updates. Hive Corps improves over time without silently rewriting business rules or bypassing approvals.

User flow

From ambiguous request to approved customer response.

  1. Trigger

    A customer submits a messy quote request.

  2. Plan

    The agent corps extracts requirements and creates a workflow plan.

  3. Decide

    Memory, catalog, pricing, and policy checks shape the quote.

  4. Approve

    Risky decisions route to a human approval gate.

  5. Output

    Hive Corps generates a customer-ready quote and proof trail.

  6. Learn

    Human edits become reviewable policy and memory proposals.

Technical architecture

Qwen orchestration with deterministic business controls.

Customer Request Hive Corps API Qwen Agent Layer Catalog + Memory Risk Gate Quote Output Audit + Learning

Qwen is used for role-specific reasoning and writing in live mode. Deterministic logic owns pricing math, approval gates, fallback behavior, and proof export so business controls remain stable.
